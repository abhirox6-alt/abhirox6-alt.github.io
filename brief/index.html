Part I — The Product & The Data

The Opportunity No One Has Taken

The way 50 million retail traders interact with markets is about to fundamentally change. Today they click through dashboards, juggle five apps, and synthesize fragmented data under time pressure with real money at stake. Every broker in the world is adding AI to this workflow, but none of them are rethinking the workflow itself. They're building faster dashboards. We're building the intelligent terminal that replaces them.

Draconic is an AI trading terminal. Four months live, 12,000 signups, 53,000+ queries — and 37.8% of those queries involve real money decisions, not browsing. Users are already trusting the AI with live positions, uploading personal trading strategies, and returning to trade through the product regularly. Weekly signups have gone from 71 to 2,815 in six weeks.

We're raising a $2-3M seed round to add engineering depth, scale acquisition, and expand into global markets.

What Draconic Actually Does

Draconic is a trading terminal reimagined around AI. It's not an analysis tool bolted onto a chat interface but a complete trading workflow where every question returns visual intelligence, analytical depth, and actionable synthesis in one conversation.

When a trader asks about a breakout setup, they don't get a paragraph of text. They get annotated charts with levels marked, real-time options intelligence, proprietary analytical signals, and a clear read on what it all means for the trade. Visual and textual, together. The response adapts to the question: candlestick charts when structure matters, custom visualizations when the data demands it, options flow when derivatives are in play.

The depth underneath comes from 3,000+ hours of derivatives trading experience encoded into proprietary frameworks like swing velocity, microstructure acceptance patterns, and market balance scoring. These metrics don't exist in any general-purpose AI because they require deep domain expertise to design, and in trading, where hallucination costs real money, the bar for accuracy is absolute.

What makes this possible is a multi-layered engineering stack that goes well beyond prompting a language model. We process raw market data across 900+ instruments down to 1-minute resolution and compute 200+ proprietary metrics in real-time, from momentum and volatility signals to options flow and microstructure patterns. On top of that sits a context engineering layer that gives the AI the equivalent of a seasoned prop trader's judgment: understanding how to read confluence across timeframes, when a setup is high-probability versus noise, how to weight conflicting signals, and how to synthesize all of this into a response that a trader can act on immediately. The agent doesn't just retrieve data; it reasons about it the way an experienced trader would, because that reasoning was built into the system layer by layer.

Users save trading strategies the AI remembers across sessions, set conditional alerts that monitor markets when they can't, and connect their existing broker accounts to execute trades without leaving the conversation. The trader stays in one place and everything else comes to them.

The product was deliberately built for a global audience from the start, serving traders across equities, derivatives, crypto, forex, and commodities, from intraday scalpers working 1-minute charts to swing traders on daily timeframes, in English and Hinglish alike. India is the launch market, but the architecture is designed to serve traders anywhere.

How trust builds inside the product.

From the 1st query to the 5th, opportunity scanning drops from 43% to 12% while trading decisions rise from 5% to 20%. Single-shot questions give way to back-and-forth dialogue where users refine their analysis, test the AI's reasoning, and build conviction before committing capital. You can see it in the raw queries. An early session might start with:

"Anything setting up on GOLD, SILVER, or CRUDE?"

By the third or fourth exchange in that same session, the questions have changed:

Nobody designed this progression. It emerged from the quality of responses. Across 4,150 conversations where users asked four or more questions, 60.3% reached an explicit trade decision moment where the user decided to act with real money.

Part II — The Story, The Market & Where We're Headed

The Flywheel That Compounds With Every Query

Every query teaches us something, and those learnings compound structurally.

When a new user asks "Should I buy Nifty 25300 CE?", the system doesn't just generate a response. It evaluates that response in real-time across 15+ parameters: relevance, accuracy, completeness, actionability, and domain-specific quality signals. If the response earns a follow-up question, that's a signal. If the user comes back the next day, that's a stronger one. If they upload their personal strategy and start managing positions through us, the product has become part of their trading workflow.

Each of these interactions adds to our understanding of what traders actually need, how they phrase it, and what kind of response earns trust versus what gets ignored. Over 53,000 queries, this has become a proprietary map of trading intent that informs everything we build. We know which types of analysis lead to repeat sessions. We know how decision-making language changes as users deepen their trust. We know what a trader needs to see before they put real money on the line.

The reason this functions as a flywheel rather than just a growing dataset is the compounding structure underneath. More users bring more diverse queries across different instruments, languages, and trading styles. More diverse queries expose edge cases and intent patterns the system hasn't seen before. The evaluation pipeline captures what works and what doesn't on every response, continuously improving quality. Better quality earns deeper trust, which brings users back and makes them bring other traders. A user signing up today benefits from everything 12,000 users before them taught the system. And the behavioral intelligence that accumulates, the patterns of how traders progress from scanning to deciding to executing, is something that can't be replicated by throwing more compute at a larger language model.

How We Got Here

Draconic wasn't always an AI company. We originally raised capital to build a discipline engine for traders. About a year ago, we pivoted to building an AI-native trading terminal, and we had to do it with the capital we already had. ~$350K in pre-seed capital, raised for a different product, now funding an AI-native trading terminal from scratch.

Building an AI company on $350K is an uphill climb. In the Indian trading space, well-funded players routinely spend more on a single content channel than our entire capital raised. At that budget you can't hire senior engineers, you can't outsource to agencies, and you can't buy your way through growth. So we built a different kind of operating model instead.

We built systems that multiply a small team's output: in-house AI agents for content creation, analytics, and operations. We went deep into AI infrastructure, context engineering, and tooling early on, while building the backend data pipelines and real-time processing infrastructure in parallel. That expertise now runs through every part of the business. We learned to use AI across operations the way most companies only use it in their product, and we moved faster than teams with ten times the headcount because the constraint demanded it.

Today our total team cost is $6,000 per month for two founders, two junior developers, a designer, and a generalist. Everything from scripts and reels to blogs and tweets gets produced through in-house content agents at near-zero marginal cost. Proactive user behavioral insights are generated automatically. The company runs on AI the same way the product does, and that operating model is now a genuine competitive advantage.

On acquisition, the same constraint-driven discipline applies. We experimented across Reddit (partnered with IndianStreetBets mods), Zerodha's trading Q&A, Twitter, Instagram reels with a professional cinema crew, freelance content creators, influencer agencies, individual influencers, SEO, Google Search, and Performance Max. The lessons came fast. Early influencer campaigns with broad targeting and lower-cost agencies gave poor returns, and we learned quickly that in niche markets, optimizing for the cheapest option consistently gives near-zero ROI even when the absolute spend is small.

The learning that changed everything was this: in niche markets, fewer and better bets win over more and cheaper ones. One high-affinity trading influencer, someone whose audience genuinely matched our product, delivered 3,000+ signups for $1,800. Google Search and Performance Max campaigns are now delivering activated users at a blended $0.90 each after several iterations on positioning and targeting. These work because the fit is real, not because the budget is big.

The growth trajectory reflects this. We launched a beta with external users in October 2025. After three months of real usage, we paused acquisition to focus on what the data was telling us: refining the analytical frameworks, enriching the visual experience, and deepening the agent's trading intelligence. Weekly signups dropped to 71 during that period. When we restarted acquisition in February, we had a better product and began systematically testing channels to find what actually scales. The trust ladder inside the product is itself a growth loop, because users who make trading decisions through Draconic naturally bring other traders. The playbook is layered: product-led growth at the core, content and education for compounding reach, and proven paid channels as accelerant.

This constraint-driven phase showed early signs of product-market fit, validated initial acquisition economics, and demonstrated what a small AI-native team can build. What it couldn't prove is scale. Frugality has a ceiling. You can't hire senior engineers at $6K/month total team cost. You can't run international acquisition experiments without real budget. And the conversion infrastructure that turns thousands of active users into predictable revenue needs dedicated engineering focus. That's where this raise comes in.

First Revenue — What We've Learned

We launched paid plans three weeks ago with a free tier (3 analyses per day, forever) and two paid tiers: Strategist at $9/month and Commander at $21/month, with INR alternatives for Indian users. We currently have 23 subscribers.

We turned monetization on early and deliberately, before building the trust infrastructure that typically drives conversion in consumer products: content that demonstrates value, social proof, demo workflows, community presence. Users are also still learning what's possible with AI in trading; many are discovering through our free tier that the product can genuinely help their decision-making, and that learning process takes a few sessions before the payment conversation even makes sense.

Given all of that, 23 users self-selecting to pay within three weeks is the signal we were testing for. And what those users do after paying matters more than how many there are: they engage at 8.36 queries per week and 2.7 sessions per week, which is 10x the intensity of free users. They're trading through the product regularly.

Early willingness to pay is there. The trust and conversion infrastructure is what we build next.

On the business model: Subscription validates willingness to pay and funds early operations, but it's the entry wedge, not the endgame. As the product evolves toward agentic workflows where AI orchestrates entire trading sessions, the volume of trades flowing through the terminal grows naturally. At that scale, transaction-linked revenue opens up naturally, which is the model that has proven to work at massive scale in trading. Beyond that, proprietary trading intelligence becomes infrastructure that brokers and platforms want to embed, putting B2B API licensing on the roadmap. And international expansion unlocks pricing at 5-10x Indian rates for the same product.

Intelligence is the wedge. Execution is the expansion. Data is the moat.

Why the Market Is Wide Open

There are 50 million active retail traders globally. India alone has 9 million in the world's largest derivatives market, growing year over year, and the global trading TAM sits at $50B with a 12% CAGR.

The trading experience hasn't been reimagined in a decade. Traders still juggle multiple apps, dozens of indicators, and fragmented information sources to make time-sensitive decisions with real money. Every major platform is attempting to add intelligence, and nobody has cracked it at depth. There's a structural reason for that: trading AI requires real-time data engineering at scale, deep domain expertise that takes years to build, and conversational UX that earns trust with real money on the line. Pure AI startups have the tech but not the trading depth. Incumbent brokers have the domain but can't rethink their UX. Institutional tools like Bloomberg serve a different user at $24K/year. The retail trader who wants all three at consumer pricing has nobody serving them globally.

The entire industry is converging on the same solution: a better dashboard. Dhan just launched DEXT, a customizable trading terminal. Groww built 915. Every broker is competing on faster data feeds, more market depth levels, and drag-and-drop customization where users arrange their own layouts and widgets. They're all optimizing the same paradigm: a human sitting at a screen, clicking through panels, executing everything themselves. The AI they add is a feature on top of this dashboard, not a rethinking of the interface itself.

None of them are building for the agentic era. When the human becomes an orchestrator who sets goals and parameters while AI agents handle the scanning, analysis, and execution, the entire UI/UX philosophy changes. Customizable dashboards become irrelevant if the agent is doing the work. The interface becomes a conversation, not a control panel. This isn't a small difference in product design; it's a fundamentally different bet on where trading goes.

Draconic has no legacy to protect. Built from scratch for active traders, with a product architecture that assumes the agent does the heavy lifting and the human provides judgment and intent. Everyone else is building the best version of the old paradigm. We're building for the new one.

Where This Is Going

The data is showing us what comes next, and it maps directly to our roadmap.

669 users uploading personal strategies are telling us they want the AI to think like them. That's Trader DNA: personalized AI that knows your style, risk tolerance, and preferred setups. 760 users managing live positions through chat are telling us they trust AI as the interface to their money. Today they connect their existing broker and execute without leaving the conversation; tomorrow intelligent agents manage the full execution workflow on their behalf. And 937 "next-day prep" queries combined with the evening activity peak tell us traders want the AI working when they're not watching, which points directly to proactive intelligence through alerts, autonomous monitoring, and pre-market briefs.

These aren't features imagined on a whiteboard. They're patterns emerging from how real traders use the product, and they point to a single destination.

The Agentic Terminal. Today's trading interface is built around humans clicking through dashboards, and while every competitor invests in making those dashboards faster and more customizable, the paradigm itself is ending. The next generation is orchestration, where the trader sets intent and parameters and intelligent agents handle the rest: scanning markets continuously, surfacing opportunities that match the trader's style, validating setups against their own risk framework, executing trades through connected brokers, managing positions in real-time, and adapting as conditions change. The trader provides judgment. The agents do the work. When the entire trading workflow runs through intelligent agents, every trade that flows through the terminal is a commercial event.

We focused on intelligence first because that's the hardest part to build and the hardest to replicate. Execution follows naturally from trust. Distribution follows from product quality. Every week that passes, the intelligence gets deeper, the user understanding gets richer, and the product gets harder to replicate.

The divide won't be retail vs institutional. It'll be augmented vs unaugmented.
